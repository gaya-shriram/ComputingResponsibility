Analysis on Scope and Availability

    •  The scope of the policy is too broad and could lead to enforcement discrepancies among countries.
  • Policy wrongly places "burden of proof" on users.
  • Policy fails to address issues of how to request erasure of cross-posted information with any clarity.
  • Responsibilities of data controllers (Facebook,Google,You Tube) are not clearly articulated.

Analysis on Impact on Free Speech

  • Right to forgot poses biggest threat to free speech in the coming decade.
  • This right could produce chilling effect caused by deletion in ambiguous cases.
  • There is an possibility of important information being erased.

Suggested Revision

  • Limit the scope of the policy so that it applies only to data that users have consented to.
  • Define the right to be forgotten in specific terms by clearly articulating situations where erasure is appropriate.
  • Address the issue of information cross-posted on multiple platforms and whether it is up to the users to ensure erasure is carried out to fullest extent.
  • State as explicitly as possible the responsibilities of data controller in terms of fulling an erasure request.
