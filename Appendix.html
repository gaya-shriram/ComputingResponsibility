As part of this class exercise i interviewed a Yahoo employee to know more about how things work at yahoo and if the "right to be forgotten" rule is being applied and his opinion about this law.

Name : Balachandar Sankar

Position : Senior Software Engineer

Company: Yahoo Inc.

The interview was in person. Balachandar was very open to discussing the work yahoo is doing in terms of maintaining the privacy of its users. Even though he could not dwell much in detail due to the company policy he was very helpful and answered all the questions i had.


List of Questions:

  • Is right to be forgotten applied on Yahoo search engine?
  • What is the procedure?
  • Success rate of the request submitted?
  • How many requests to they receive?
  • Any such act on US site?
  • Form availability?
  • His opinion about this right?

Above are few questions from the interview. I got to know the law is in place in Europe. The form, details on how to fill it and other details on the right is available on the yahoo website. The rate of request is almost similar to Google and strict procedure is carried out in reviewing the request. He feels that the rule should be brought into all the other countries and it is the individuals right to decide when and where his information should be present.
